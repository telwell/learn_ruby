How the Course Works

Basically, it's all the best parts of taking an in-person class with your friends and the flexibility of working online from home.

You’ll be part of a small team of students who progress through the 16-week program together. With 24 hour chat and nearly constant video access to instructors, you won’t spend long scratching your head if you get stuck.

Our program is built with the understanding that some people have committed to learning full time and others need to juggle an existing career. Though we cover a significant amount of material, you should be able to complete your week’s work in roughly 30 hours. Our daily check-ins are scheduled outside of normal work hours.

The Curriculum

You will learn everything you need to begin your new career as a developer.

It starts before you're even admitted -- our prep work mini-courses get you up to speed on important topics like development basics, web design, engineering, and coding fundamentals. After you're enrolled, we work with you to make sure you're firing on all cylinders by day 1.

Once the program begins, each week will cover a new set of topics. You'll learn more than a dozen major languages, frameworks, and technologies from tutorials, custom video lessons, project demos, and code reviews before building all kinds of projects to show off what you can do.

Throughout the course, we'll give you guidance on how to transition into a career as a developer. We have speakers from the industry provide their perspective and teach you everything from managing your digital presence to finding your place in the developer community. The end of the program is our "hiring bootcamp", where we guide you through the application, interview, and onboarding process.

You Will Learn:

  • How to approach and break down problems like an engineer
  • Object-oriented programming with Ruby, the language built for programmer happiness
  • Designing effective systems architecture and implementing data models with SQL
  • Building and deploying well-tested web applications using the Ruby on Rails framework
  • Using Git version control to pair program with other developers and collaborate within a team
  • Building great looking websites with well structured and semantic HTML and CSS
  • Leveraging front end frameworks to save time and create mobile-friendly user experiences
  • Using Javascript and jQuery to make websites interactive and responsive
  • Creating buttery smooth experiences by using AJAX to communicate with APIs
  • How to market yourself effectively and nail technical interviews so you can kick start your career.

FAQ

How will I get a job after this course?

We will provide you with the core components you need to get a job as a web developer: the technical skills, proficiency with the tools of the trade, a professional workflow, a kick start for your portfolio, relationships with mentors and the engineering foundations you'll need to keep learning.

Over the course of the program, we work with you to make sure your job search will be productive. We cover everything from managing your web presence to building an effective resume to working with search firms to interviewing at companies. Even after the program has concluded, we'll do everything we can to make sure you're pointed in the right direction for the next step in your career.

Why take this program over other programs?

In-person programs require you to quit your job and move halfway across the country. An online solution offers significantly more flexibility.

But other online programs either cover just a fraction of the content or they only let you interact with other humans a couple of times a week. We believe that community and support are some of the best reasons for doing an in-person program but no one else is getting it right online.

That's why we don't just surround you with other students, instructors, and mentors, but actually have you collaborate on projects with them directly. This level of interaction between students isn't being done online by anybody else.

Even more importantly, our admissions process ensures that your teammates are as motivated, talented, and creative as yourself. This allows us to get extremely deep into the material and move at a pace which will take you much further than other programs typically do. When you're looking for a job, this becomes an important advantage.

How much does the program cost?

The cost for the program is $6800.

We require an $800 non-refundable deposit upon acceptance to reserve your place in the cohort and the remaining balance must be paid in full one week prior to your start date.

Is there a payment plan available?

We have an installment plan and several other choices. Reach out if you'd like more information about the available options.

How does the application process work?

Our application process is designed to make sure that your other classmates are as exceptional as you are. It consists of the application and an interview. The interview is conducted via Skype (or Google Hangouts).

The interview will test your ability to solve problems using code. The goal isn't to trip you up with syntax but to hear how you think through a challenging problem and communicate your solution. It's okay if you're a beginner -- we provide you with the resources you need to get up to speed on Ruby beforehand.

Why are you teaching Ruby/Rails/Javascript?

There's no wrong language to start with, but some are more right than others. Ruby is an excellent language to start with because it was literally "designed for programmer happiness" so you can focus on understanding the fundamentals of object-oriented programming instead of getting stuck in the idiosyncrasies of the language itself. Its extremely powerful and the community around it is like none other so it's very easy to locate help when you need it.

Ruby also gives you access to Rails, one of the most popular and productive web development frameworks in the world. It's used by everyone from fast moving startups to full-fledged enterprise software companies. We chose Rails because it has a very strong community, it's widely used, and it's an opinionated way to learn a full-featured MVC application flow.

The back portion of our program focuses entirely on Javascript. The language isn't quite as straightforward as Ruby, but it's used in every browser in the world and it spreads further every day. A deep understanding of Javascript unlocks far more than just the ability to build dynamic pages -- it allows you to construct full single-page web applications using modern frameworks like Backbone, Angular, or Ember and full-stack applications by incorporating Node, Meteor and the like.

Learning all of these languages is well worth it -- you get a number of different perspectives on programming and building web applications which will give you a highly versatile capability. They're each in-demand skills and their combination will prepare you to learn or build pretty much anything.

Can I learn iOS/Python/PHP/etc after the course?

This course is designed to give you a rock solid foundation in software engineering. Though our core technologies are Ruby-on-Rails and Javascript, the fundamentals you'll learn are a great starting point from which to expand your knowledge into other areas.

During the program, you learn a new language or significant technology almost every single week. More importantly, you learn how to learn. Picking up additional technologies after the program will be significantly easier than before and the world of technology is your oyster.

Can I build a startup after this course?

Short answer: that depends on you.

We have chosen Ruby on Rails as our core web technology because it is one of the easiest to learn and one of the fastest to launch. If you work hard to understand the theoretical concepts, really throw yourself into the material, and practice the engineering mentality we encourage, then where you take it next is a wide open door.

Is it too late to learn web development?

NO!

We hear things like “I thought that ship had sailed” all the time. It’s a common misconception that you have to be a certain age to learn web development for the first time. We've seen students from 17 to 70 succeed.

What does a typical week look like?

We firmly believe in a "flipped classroom" model where you can learn at your own pace and then apply it by building projects with the support and supervision of classmates and instructors.

You will start your week by reading material and watching videos to get up to speed on the week’s topic. You’ll also dive into a series of exercises that hammer home the core concepts. These can be done individually or by collaborating with your classmates.

You’ll take on the week’s project with one of your classmates. The projects are managed using a typical Agile Development workflow and are due by Sunday night. There will be periodic quizzes to make sure you’re keeping up with the material.

I live in a non-US time zone, is this okay?

Most of our students are currently in the US and so our program is designed to accommodate the way students in US time zones work. You will need to partner with other students on projects and scheduling that becomes difficult between largely different time zones. While we're open to students from around the world, you would need to be mindful of those issues until we add international cohorts.

How can I get help when I need it?

You’ll have live access to your classmates at all times and instructors and TAs will be available to answer questions via video and chat throughout the week. During project periods they'll work with you to make sure you're on the right track and they're available for 1-on-1 meetings whenever you need them.

We strongly believe in collaborative learning, so we encourage you to work with other students on the assignments and require it for the projects. It's hard to learn faster than when you're building a project with someone else!

What happens after the course?

After you've finished the program and gone through our hiring bootcamp, you’ll get continued access to the course materials. You'll also be enrolled in our alumni portal, where former students can exchange everything from ideas, to job tips to collaborating on future products.

What do I need in order to make the course work?

You only need an Internet connection and reliable access to a computer capable of using video chat and installing the necessary software. A Mac is strongly recommended, followed by a Linux setup. A Windows machine is really not ideal. If you're serious about getting into web development, you should really look into getting a Mac.

Should I do the prep work before applying?

The prep mini-courses are freely available to anyone who wants to learn from them. They will certainly help you to gain a better understanding of how to build successful web applications but they are not required before you submit your application.

That said, we do encourage you to run through them (especially the last course on coding) prior to doing your interview to make sure you're at the right level to succeed.

We expect enrolled students to have completed the prep work prior to beginning their cohort.

How long do the prep courses take?

The free prep mini-courses are designed to take 1-2 weeks each to complete. They are meant to be completed in order from Web Development Basics to Web Markup and Coding but...

If you're in a rush (for instance to prepare for your interview), focus on the final mini-course called Web Markup and Coding. It covers HTML/CSS and Ruby programming, the most immediately applicable skills to your interview and your time in the program.

What happens if I need to leave the course?

If you need to leave the course, you'll have the option of enrolling in a future cohort or withdrawing. If you withdraw, you'll be returned a prorated tuition amount based on the weeks you've attended.

If you are asked to leave the course for disciplinary or performance reasons, you will be treated as having withdrawn.
