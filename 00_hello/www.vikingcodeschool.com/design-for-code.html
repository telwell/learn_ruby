Design for Code

You're a wiz with design. We're geeks about code. We have much to discuss!

Here's the deal...

We're really good at teaching people to code. It's what we love and it's why almost all our time is spent building a great product around it. Unfortunately, we need to generate assets to support all the awesome stuff we've got coming in the future but we don't have core expertise in design. That's where you come in.

If you're a talented designer and illustrator and you want to level up your skills by learning to code, we've got an offer for you:

If you help us with design, we'll teach you how to code

The details of this will be figured out case-by-case, but we run a highly valuable online program geared towards bringing advanced beginners to an employable level in full stack web development. These skills can put rocket boosters on your abilities going forward and officially make you a Unicorn.

For consideration or more info, send your portfolio and a brief description of why you rock to hello@vikingcodeschool.com.

More Details

What it looks like:

  • You'll develop a consistent visual language for our new and regularly produced content.
  • You'll create the visual assets (graphics and illustrations) to accompany that content. We have code and we have Vikings... <insert creativity here>
  • We're looking for someone who will work with us directly on a part-time (or possibly fulltime) basis. If the relationship works out beyond the skills-trade phase, we'll obviously be interested in continuing it on a more permanent (paid) level.
  • You would start ASAP. If this post is up, the position's open.

What we're looking for:

  • You are hungry to learn and succeed. While we prefer people with a proven track record, we also respect the hell out of hustle.
  • You understand the learn-to-code space and have a strong grounding in commercial web design and illustration.
  • You work very hard but don't mind getting a bit silly in the margins.
  • You communicate very well. You want to make meaningful changes in education.
  • Preference if you can pass our technical screen and participate full time in our web development program when it runs.
  • Preference for SF/Bay Area locals and those who can commit to full time (though part time and remote is okay).

Design is difficult and we respect its value as well as the process required to produce it. If you don't join us, we'll be forced to use stock photography, which, as we all know, causes a rainbow-shooting flying toaster to lose its wings. Do you want that on your conscience?

You can learn more about our program here, but note that it will shortly be changing in some meaningful and exciting ways.

Send your portfolio and a brief description of why you're awesome to hello@vikingcodeschool.com

If you know someone who might be interested, please share this with them!
